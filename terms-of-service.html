5. Storage, Usage & Safe Consumption

⚠ Important — Please Follow These Guidelines

Failure to store and use ghee correctly may compromise its quality and safety. We cannot accept liability for any adverse outcome resulting from improper storage or use.

  • Storage: Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Keep the jar tightly sealed after use. Do not refrigerate unless temperatures exceed 30°C consistently. Ideal storage temperature is 10°C–25°C.
  • Moisture contamination: Always use a clean, dry spoon. Never introduce water or wet utensils into the jar. Moisture is the primary cause of rancidity in pure ghee.
  • Shelf life: Best consumed within 12 months of manufacture when stored correctly. The manufacture and best-before dates are printed on the jar lid. Do not consume after the best-before date.
  • Signs of spoilage: Discard the product if you notice an unusual sour smell, discolouration, or visible mould. Do not taste-test suspected spoiled ghee.
  • Cross-contamination: Keep away from strong-smelling foods and chemicals. Ghee is porous to odours.
  • Children: Ghee is generally safe for children over 6 months of age when introduced appropriately. Consult your paediatrician before introducing ghee to infants.
